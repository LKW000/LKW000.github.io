What is Harlem Renaissance?

The Harlem Renaissance was a cultural and artistic phenomenon for African-Americans in the 1920s and 1930s. The event is called Harlem Renaissance because the culture and arts began to flourish in Harlem, New York. Harlem Renaissance is an important cultural movement because the movement gave African Americans a voice and a place in Western culture.

The Harlem Renaissance helped black people develop a new sense of pride, liberty, and commitment to political activism. While the Artists, singers, leaders, dancers, and writers rejected the stereotypes made by white people. The Harlem Renaissance produced many forms of artistic masterpiece, showing what it means to be black. Harlem Renaissance influenced black culture, inspiring the future generation of black artists, writers, musicians, and more.

African American was given a new mindset, feeling a feeling of pride and community. Thanks to the Harlem Renaissance, the foundation was provided for the Civil Rights Movement of the 1950s and 1960s. While it is flourishing, it validated the perspective of the founders and leaders like Alain Locke and Langston Hughes that any form of art can improve the lives of black people.
